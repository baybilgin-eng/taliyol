Vekse Rock Churches, Rock Defense City, Rock Palace

Vekse, or Özlüce as it is now called, is an ancient village located on the southern slope of the Akbin Valley, in the central part of the valley, within the Gesi district of Kayseri. On the northern slope of the valley, opposite the village, lie the remains of ancient civilizations. In this article, I have compiled the artifacts and structures we encountered during our surface survey.

Vekse Hıdırellez Church: This church is entirely carved out of rock. Built on a vantage point overlooking the valley, its entrance faces north. Once inside, the structure extends to the left. The interior architecture is remarkable; the carved niches are remarkably well-preserved. We can discern from the remaining 5-10 cm² frescoes that the entire church's walls were once covered in them.

Vekse North Church 1: This church is also entirely carved out of rock, and its entrance faces south, towards Mount Erciyes. The carved niches inside the church are extremely well-made. We see a rock inscription dated 1909 at its entrance, and we understand from the handful of frescoes that remain that the entire walls of this church were once covered with frescoes.

Vekse North Church 2: This church, with its stone-walled entrance and a pierced roof transformed into a birdhouse, resembles other churches in the area. Facing south, we were able to see only a handful of frescoes inside.

Vekse Rock Palace: Easily distinguishable from all other structures by its intricate architectural craftsmanship when viewed from a distance, we have given this building the name "palace." Inside, there are several interconnected, tall, and regularly shaped rock chambers. The structure is surrounded by a thick wall.

Vekse Kaya Defense City: Dozens of interconnected rooms, a vantage point overlooking the valley, observation windows, and tunnels stretching endlessly and branching out led us to call this structure a defense city. We couldn't understand the purpose of the rock cavities in the tunnel floors.
