Seven Churches

The Seven Churches are located in the village of Yukarı Bakraçlı, on the western slope of Mount Erek. Dating back to the 8th century, this structure, as its name suggests, consists of seven churches (it's worth noting that this is not entirely clear; some sources mention five, others three, and the reason for the name "Seven Churches" remains a mystery, as its original name wasn't like that). Several variations exist regarding the name used during the Armenian era: Varagavank, Warak Wank, Varak Surp Harç Monastery, St. Cross Monastery of Varak, etc. We know that "Varak" is the Armenian name for Mount Erek, and "Vank" means church. While it's possible to translate it as "Mount Erek Church" in Turkish, it's interestingly called "Seven Churches," but if we asked anyone to show us the seven churches, no one could.

Although we call it a church, it's actually a monastery composed of churches; there are very old photos of the monastery on the internet, it's a complex of buildings surrounded by thick and high walls.

It should also be noted that the monastery was home to the Archbishop of Van and was a relatively wealthy monastery.

The church is easily accessible, located 10 km from the city. There is an asphalt road leading all the way to the Seven Churches. The village of Yukarı Bakraçlı, where the Seven Churches are located, is surrounded by greenery and has abundant freshwater sources. It is built on a high and commanding point that offers a bird's-eye view of Van city center.

Sadly, the church is now abandoned and on the verge of collapse. Its door is locked, the key is in the village, but you can ask them to open it for you; at least in this way, it's being preserved.

Upon entering the church, you realize its magnificent structure; it boasts breathtaking stonework, frescoes are still visible on the walls, albeit faded, and the symbols carved into the rock are remarkably well-preserved.
