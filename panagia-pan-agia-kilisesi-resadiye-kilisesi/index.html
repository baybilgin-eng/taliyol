Kayseri & Koramaz · 6 May 2011

Panagia (Pan Agia) Church (Reşadiye Church)

Reşadiye is a neighborhood in the Talas district of Kayseri. I learned from local residents that its old name was İstefana. Although it is officially named Erciyes Township today, its original name is Reşadiye. The area is situated on the slope of the Derevenk Valley, at the foot of Mount Ali. What makes Reşadiye the subject of my writing is the presence of a church called Panagia (Pan Agia) here. Both its architecture and accounts from locals confirm that it is a Greek Orthodox church. I believe the church was actively used until the Greeks left the area (approximately 1924). After the Greeks left, the church was first used as a school, and then from 1935 onwards, as a textile factory. The factory, which began production under the name Erciyes Textile Factory, was founded by Seyit Ali Küçükdeveci, Mehmet Küçükdeveci, and Ahmet İstanbulluoğlu. More detailed information about the factory can be found at this address: ( http://www.facebook.com/topic.php?uid=59044758784&topic=6736 ).

I must say that the church is in extremely poor condition due to its use as a factory. Because the existing church was inadequate, additional structures were built, and apparently, electricity was generated there using diesel engines. The factory, which operated until 1975, had its siren removed and transferred to the Erciyes Municipality.

The church is accessed from Reşadiye Square; located on the southern slope of the square, it offers a direct view of Mount Ali and Mount Erciyes. A water cistern is situated to the east of the church; it is believed to have been filled with snow in winter and fed by flowing water in summer. Only a few frescoes have survived on the church walls; these frescoes are included in the photographs below.
