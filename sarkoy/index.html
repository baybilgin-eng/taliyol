Kayseri & Koramaz · 14 April 2011

Sharkoy

The ancient settlement of Şarköy is located in the Tufanbeyli district of Adana, at the intersection of the Adana and Kayseri provincial borders. Currently, Şarköy, an Avşar village, is situated directly on top of the ancient settlement. This has resulted in significant damage and even loss of historical structures. Examination of the houses reveals that many of them use stones salvaged from the original buildings in their walls.

A river flows through the area, giving it life. There is a marble gate, 5-6 meters high, adorned with finely carved motifs. The area also contains a church and an amphitheater. Examination of the structures clearly reveals that they were constructed from block stone and marble, indicating the presence of a great civilization in the region.

Now, let's move on to what I've learned from the local people and from the internet:

Şarköy, formerly known as Komana, is said to have been the site of a goddess named Ma-Enyo, whose hair was part silver and part black. She was considered a symbol of femininity, and thousands of women and eunuchs served her. Şarköy is said to be the location of her temple. The enormous marble gate that still stands today was the entrance to that temple. The temple and the goddess were so revered that all the kings of the region would visit it just before their coronations. Each visit offered the goddess very rich gifts. According to legends, when it rained, villagers would gather along the riverbank to collect the gold that was unearthed by the rainwater erosion. The temple, burned after wars, is now buried several meters underground due to powerful landslides in the region. In the 1940s, British researchers discovered that a complete ancient city lay beneath the site.

Everything I've described above I've either heard or read online. My only opinion about whether it's true is that it's positive. Every time I go there, I'm filled with awe and a strange feeling; it's a very mystical place.
