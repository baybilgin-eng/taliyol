Kayseri & Koramaz · 5 June 2015

Historic Shahrukh Bridge

The sign next to the bridge reads:

“Historical Shahruk (Shahruk) Bridge: Built in 897 AH/1492 AD by Shahrukh Bey, son of Ala'üddevle, from the Zülkadir lineage, during the Dulkadirid Principality, the bridge is 144.56 meters long, 5.6 meters wide, and has 8 arches.”

The bridge, which was repaired by Mehmet Han, son of Şahruh Bey, in 945 AH / 1538/39 AD, was later repaired again in 1908, 1935, and 1957. Repair and landscaping work, initiated by the Highways 6th Regional Directorate in 1997, was completed in 1999 and the bridge was opened to service.

The exact location of the bridge is given below, and it can be reached by taking the Kayseri – Sivas highway towards Sarıoğlan and passing Sarıoğlan.

39 D 11′ 00” N

35 D 56′ 16” E
