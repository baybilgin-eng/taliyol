Little Aydos – Three Hills Region

Located in the triangle formed by Güzelköy (Nize), Turan, and Bürüngüz, this area, which is clearly different when viewed from satellite, emerges as a very interesting tourist destination.

The landforms and rock structure are quite different from the standard rock structure of Kayseri.

You can access the area using several different routes; for example, you can easily reach the area by passing through Güzelköy.

In this area you will encounter the following:

1- Tumuli: There are two tumuli side-by-side on a dominant hill in the area; one of them has been previously excavated and you can enter it, you can see images of it below.

2- Water-eroded limestone cliffs and caves: The area has numerous small valleys and cliffs, where you will find dozens of caves of varying sizes.

3- Ox-cart routes: The region was once a route used by numerous ox-carts, as you can still see deep ox-cart wheel tracks on the ground.

4- Ancient building remains: The area contains numerous neatly cut stone fragments, broken pottery, broken arches, etc., whose history and ownership are unknown.

5-Turan Village Fountain: This fountain has incredibly cold and delicious water, but it's more than just a fountain. The area is home to numerous walnut, plum, apple, apricot, pear, poplar, and willow trees. Located in the narrowest part of a valley, it's like an oasis in the desert, especially during the summer heat.

6-Ancient cistern-like structure: I say cistern-like because it only resembles one; I don't think it's a cistern. It has a long chimney and an entrance at the bottom. If anyone has any information or ideas about what this structure is and would like to share them, I would be grateful. There is another similar structure directly opposite it.

7- Küçük Aydosun Fountain: This fountain, with its ice-cold water, is located at the mouth of a valley. It is surrounded by numerous trees, and the valley where the fountain is situated contains many caves.
