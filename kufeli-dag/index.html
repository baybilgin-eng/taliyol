Kefeli Mountain

This mountain, rising on the northwestern slope of Erciyes, is within the borders of Hacılar district. After entering the road leading from Hacılar to Süt Donduran Plateau, you turn left at the first fork in the road. This road will take you to the foothills of the mountain. In the summer months, the shepherds from Hurmetci village settle in the foothills. Climbing the mountain directly via a steep route is not advisable; if you climb horizontally along the eastern slope, you will see a path that winds to the back of the mountain. This path will also take you to the inner plateaus of Erciyes. I saw upside-down tulips on the summit in May. There are also a few poplar trees and dwarf pines, typical of the region, on the summit. This mountain, offering a superb view of the city and Mount Erciyes, is a must-see. I measured its summit at 2600 meters.
