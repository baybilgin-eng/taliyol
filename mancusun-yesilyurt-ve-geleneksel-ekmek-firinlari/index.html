Mancusun (Yeşilyurt) and Traditional Bread Bakeries

Our journey took us to what is now called Yeşilyurt Neighborhood, formerly known to the locals as Mancusun.

With its narrow streets and stone houses built during the Ottoman period, it is a charming village located in Gesi, Kayseri.

A charming village where, while wandering its streets, you might feel as if you're stepping back into the 14th century.

I saw that the women of this village have been continuing their tradition of baking their own bread for centuries, using methods that have remained unchanged.

Stone-built wood-fired ovens, pre-fermented bread dough, and the wonderful aroma of warm, rustic bread 30 minutes after the oven is lit.

The veteran women I met in Mancusun told me that they had learned how to bake bread this way for hundreds of years, having passed it down from their mothers.

However, bread isn't baked in every home or every bakery every day, so if you're as lucky as I am, follow the wonderful smell of warm bread wafting down the street.
