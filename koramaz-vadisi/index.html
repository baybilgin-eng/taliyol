Kayseri & Koramaz · 14 April 2011

Koramaz Valley

The valley, located within the boundaries of Kayseri's Subaşı, Küçük Bürüngüz, Ağırnas, Turan, Vekse, and Bağpınar neighborhoods, bears traces of ancient civilizations. A stream flows through the valley, which stretches from Subaşı to the Kayseri-Sivas highway. The stream is fed by freshwater springs originating at various points in the valley and by collected water from the surrounding area.

The valley contains numerous caves of varying sizes, aviaries, columbariums, churches, and underground defensive structures. Some of these caves still contain frescoes. These caves, once used as chapels, generally face south, and their interiors feature apses facing east. The valley is home to a predominantly fruit-growing tree population, mainly willow and walnut trees. Vegetable and fruit gardens cultivated by the villagers are also located within the valley.

The Koramaz Valley stretches in an east-west direction, with Koramaz Mountain located at its easternmost point. Koramaz Mountain houses the analog television transmitters of Kayseri province.

The Ağırnas underground city, located in Neslihan Park, is situated within the same valley and serves as a harbinger of other underground cities in the valley.

The valley, a popular destination for hikers and nature enthusiasts, is a must-see natural treasure.

You can see Koramaz Valley on Google Maps.
