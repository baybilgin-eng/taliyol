Kevenli (Shushanis) Village

The village, known today as Kevenli but called Şuşanıs (originally Şuşants) by the locals, is located east of Van, at the foot of Mount Erek, overlooking the city.

Shushan (lily), the daughter of King Gagik Ardzruni I, who ruled in Van in the 1st century, built a famous monastery here. The word Shushants means "lily" in Armenian.

Observant eyes looking from the city center towards the village will see a rock formation resembling a pyramid in the distance. Below you can see photographs of this rock, which is actually just an ordinary rock.

The village's abandoned old mosque is also among the places worth seeing.
