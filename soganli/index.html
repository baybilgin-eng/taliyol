Cappadocia · 23 May 2011

Bulbous

Soğanlı is a settlement in the Yeşilhisar district of Kayseri. Located in an area that could be called the "back gateway to Cappadocia," it is home to numerous churches of varying sizes. Notable churches such as the Snake Church and the Domed Church make the region a popular tourist destination. The area has the classic Cappadocian geography, and the Soğanlı River flows through it, along with a dam. Situated in a valley, the settlement bears the earliest traces of Christianity.

Speaking of Soğanlı, it's impossible not to mention the rag dolls. Made for years by the women of the region, these dolls have become a symbol of the area. They are entirely handmade by the local women and come in various sizes: small, large, depicting a bride, a woman carrying a water jug, or a woman spinning yarn. The small ones are priced at 5 TL, and the large ones at 10 TL. It's fortunate that these dolls, which reflect the culture and history of the region, have been preserved and are still produced by the locals.
