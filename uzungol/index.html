Uzungol

The journey to Uzungöl from Central Anatolia is long and tiring.

The entire road is paved, but it can only be accessed via Trabzon.

Apparently, Uzungöl can also be reached via Bayburt; I haven't tried that route, but I've heard that minibuses always use it.

There are many Arab tourists in the lake region; 90% of the hotels are full of Arab tourists. As a result, even the signs are now written in Arabic.

There is a lot of construction around the lake, the natural beauty of the lake has been completely destroyed, and naturally, there is a significant amount of pollution in the lake.

There are so many buildings by the lake that you can't even get close to it.

The plateaus above are more beautiful than the lake, and the waterfalls are worth seeing.

Hotels are relatively cheap, but the accommodations aren't very good; the rooms are generally very small, and there are a lot of flies at night.

The food in the region isn't very good either.
