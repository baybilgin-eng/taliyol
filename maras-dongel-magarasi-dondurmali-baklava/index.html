Kayseri & Koramaz · 2 November 2015

Maraş, Döngel Cave, Baklava with Ice Cream

I will share my travel notes from my trip from Kayseri to Maraş here. The journey is 260 km long, and the road is entirely dual carriageway except for the Tekir region. Numerous tunnels have been completed along the route. The route starts from Kayseri, goes to Pınarbaşı, then to Sarız, and finally to Maraş via Göksun.

Don't miss the opportunity to try the skewers at Kıskaçlı Et Lokantası (Kılıçkaya Restaurant, on the right side of the road) located between Pınarbaşı and Sarız. 50 km from Maraş lies the Döngel Cave and picnic area, a truly exquisite natural wonder that must be seen. You can have a picnic and drink the ice-cold water flowing from the caves, which are enormous. The caves are located above the picnic area and, frankly, there isn't a very easy way to access them. You need a rope to enter the caves; it's not possible to explore them on foot.

In Maraş, we had dinner at Yeşilvadi Restaurant, located in the upper part of the city. It has a children's playground, and the restaurant is situated within a forest. After dinner, we decided to try baklava with ice cream since we were in Maraş. We learned that the most famous place is Tatlıcı Yaşar, the birthplace of Mado. You absolutely must try their baklava with ice cream; there's nothing quite like it. Before you leave, don't forget to buy some ice cream with dry ice added, depending on how long your trip lasts.
