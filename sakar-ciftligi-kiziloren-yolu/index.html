Sakar Farm – Kızılören Road

Yes, in this article I will tell you about a road. What makes this road worth writing about is that it passes through the middle of a forest full of trees, more than we are used to seeing in Kayseri, and it also runs alongside a small lake.

First, let me describe how to get to the road. There's a large dog kennel located in the Sakar farm area, and this kennel is the starting point of the road. The road is approximately 10 km long and ends at Kızılören village, and then İncesu. The entire road is paved, but I can't say it's in good condition; you need to be very careful, especially during rainy periods.

Along the way, you'll be accompanied by an astonishing forest; I wouldn't have believed that Kayseri was home to such forests if I hadn't seen it with my own eyes. The forest doesn't have a great variety of trees; it's predominantly poplar and oak. The area is spread across the western slopes of Mount Erciyes, and the animal diversity is quite good compared to the rest of Kayseri. Wild boars, skunks, squirrels, wolves, and, according to rumors, lynxes are known to live in the forest.

Villagers in the area complain about wild boars, claiming they destroy their crops, and they frequently hunt them. The villagers are generally livestock farmers and graze their animals in these forests; it is possible to see numerous herds along the way.

As you can see in the photos below, there's also a small lake along the way. The lake always has water, summer or winter, and fish live in it. I think the water is collected by pooling. The lake, which is completely muddy in color, is surrounded by two vineyards. It's a perfect place for a picnic and to fill your lungs with fresh air. Forests behind you, a lake in front of you, a barbecue nearby – what more could you want?
