Mount Nemrut and the King Statues

When Mount Nemrut is mentioned, the first thing that comes to mind are the statues of kings. This is quite natural, because when you go and see them, you will understand that these are truly unique statues.

To reach Mount Nemrut, you first go to Adıyaman, then to Kahta, and from there you enter the road to Mount Nemrut National Park. The road is long but quite good. In winter, it's impossible to climb up when there's snow on the ground; even in June, we couldn't reach the summit where the king's statues are located. (It's necessary to ask the national park authorities beforehand if the summit road is open.)

The statues of the kings are positioned on the summit of Mount Nemrut, facing east, and the sunrise from this point is breathtaking; it feels as if the sun is rising right beneath your feet. I strongly recommend that everyone watch the sunrise from here at least once before they die. I was so impressed that whenever I want to imagine something beautiful, the statues and the reddish sun I saw are the first things that come to mind.

Of course, watching the sunrise from among the king's statues isn't so easy; you have to wake up 2 or 3 hours before sunrise, climb the mountain road by car in pitch darkness, and you can't drive all the way to the base of the statues, so you have to park the car 150 meters down and climb on foot. On top of all that, add the strong, bone-chilling wind.

The statues of the king are enormous and crafted with exquisite workmanship, while there are also relatively smaller statues on the western side of the summit, overlooking the sunset.

Nemrut Mountain is surrounded on all sides by artifacts that symbolize civilization; one could spend days exploring the mountain and its artifacts without seeing them all.
