Yedisalkım (Put or Pût) Village

Yedisalkım (formerly known as Put or Pût) village, located approximately 85 km from Van city center, can be reached in about 1.5 hours by car. The road leading to the village is fully paved and accessible by car. To reach the village, one turns onto a signposted road just before arriving at Güzelsu (Hoşap).

The old name of Yedisalkım village is pronounced as Put or Pût. The Başet canyon, located to the west of the village, has a very beautiful view and is home to dozens of caves of varying sizes. The most famous of these caves is the one called "The Girls' Cave," which contains cave paintings dating back to prehistoric times. This cave is located approximately 1 km from the nearest house in the village and is about 80 meters above the canyon floor. There is no path leading to the cave, and access to it is impossible without special equipment.

The village also has an old church that is now covered by houses; stones removed from the church walls are now used as repurposed stone in the walls of many houses.

The ice-cold water flowing from the village fountain is believed to come from the summit of Mount Başet, located west of the village, and considered sacred.

The village currently has approximately 40 households, an active school providing primary and secondary education, and about 20 students. The villagers largely make a living from livestock farming.

The village has electricity, but no telephone line; GSM signals reach the village, but internet access is only at the Edge level.

While walking around the village, I didn't come across a single trash can, which was quite valuable as it showed that all the waste in the village was natural.
