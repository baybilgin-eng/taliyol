Kayseri & Koramaz · 19 April 2011

Mount Lifos

Lifos, rising at the foot of Mount Erciyes, has an altitude of approximately 2510 m. Numerous poplar trees line its northern slopes. Walking among these dense trees can be impossible in places, but they are extremely beneficial in preventing landslides.

Lifos can be climbed directly from the north face, but this route is extremely challenging due to the steepness of the mountain; therefore, I recommend the south face. The south face climbing route can be reached by taking the road from Erciyes down to Hacılar and then onto the Serçer Plateau road. A few families camp on the southern slopes of Lifos during the summer months. Reaching the summit via the south route is easier because the path is shorter. There is also a freshwater spring on the southern slopes of the mountain, and this water is carried to the Serçer Plateau.

Lifos Mountain actually has two peaks, and historical ruins are located on these peaks. As you can see in the photos below, there were structures here at one time. It's very difficult to determine their purpose; frankly, I couldn't find much information on this. However, from what I heard from the locals, there were hot springs on the mountain's peak at one time, and the people of that era built baths there. Some of the structures resemble castle walls; they looked like watchtowers to me, but I'm not sure. If you have any information on this subject, I would appreciate it if you would share it.

There is also a summit register at the summit, and it would be a shame to leave without writing a memento in it, with the Turkish flag waving beside it.

I haven't encountered any animals on the mountain during my climbs so far, but I've heard from the nomadic shepherds living in the area that there are many wolves and snakes in the mountains.

Currently, cable car construction work is underway in and around Lifos. This work, which will damage the natural environment of the region, is intended to attract more people. Using the roads built for this construction, it is possible to travel from behind Lifos all the way to the Müjgar plateau.

Lifos Mountain is a wonderful place to enjoy the city view, get one step closer to Mount Erciyes, explore interesting historical structures, and fill your lungs with the scent of spring.
