Kayseri & Koramaz · 21 June 2011

Sızır Waterfall

We set off from Kayseri, entered the Sivas road, first reached Gemerek, then turned left from within Gemerek and continued towards Yeni Çubuk, and after Yeni Çubuk, Sızır extended its hand to us.

Sızır is a small settlement, and its most important tourist attraction is Sızır Waterfall. Its waters flow vigorously in the spring, gradually decreasing towards autumn, but the waterfall cascades in every season. Beautiful landscaping has been done around the waterfall, and a tea garden has been built to serve as a cafe. Sızır Waterfall is a lovely place to go to cool off during the scorching summer heat. A wooden bridge has been built at the foot of the waterfall, and getting wet from the spray while crossing it is a very enjoyable experience.

A hydroelectric power plant has also been built on the Sızır River, and this investment, which will bring economic vitality to the region, has been very appropriate. Let's also note that it is possible to reach the Çat forests via the Sızır River.
