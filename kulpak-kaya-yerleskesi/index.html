Kulpak Ancient Rock Settlement

Kulpak village can be reached by turning north from Çayırözü village on the highway connecting Develi district to İncesu district. It is also said that the original name of Kulpak village was Gülipak. You can drive to the fields and pond located north of Kulpak village, but unfortunately, there is no paved road beyond the village. After parking your car near the pond, if you look west, you can see carved windows and doors on a large rock block a few kilometers away. This structure you see is an ancient settlement.

We believe it is an ancient settlement, possibly even dating back to the pre-writing era. The settlement is situated on a slope at the intersection of three valleys, creating a tremendous echo. The entrance gate faces east, offering a panoramic view of the entire Develi plain and the Sultan Sazlığı wetlands.

The doors and windows of the building are intricately carved, and the rooms are interconnected internally; staircases and other structures are also visible. Much of the building has collapsed over time, making access to the upper floors almost impossible. We have determined that the building originally had six floors.

The passages within the ancient settlement were accomplished via steep, double-pillared tunnels. Villagers say there are relief carvings of a camel and flowers on the walls, but we couldn't identify them. We did find a few recently added cross markings in some places.

Finally, let's mention that there is also a water cistern, 5-6 meters deep, with smooth plastering.
