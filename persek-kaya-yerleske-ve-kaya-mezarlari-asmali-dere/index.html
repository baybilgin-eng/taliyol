Kayseri & Koramaz · 30 December 2017

Panli Memorial Tomb

While driving on the Kayseri – Pınarbaşı road, we turn onto the road leading to Bünyan. After the fork, we enter the first settlement we encounter, following the sign for the Panlı Monumental Tomb. This road leads directly to Panlı village and the Panlı Monumental Tomb. You can easily drive up to 500 meters from the tomb, but the rest is on foot. The monument is located on the eastern slope of Panlı village, offering a panoramic view of the village. The tomb still stands intact; according to some sources, it was an "altar," but I found no traces of any civilization on it (no inscriptions, pictures, etc.). If anyone has information about which civilization it was built in or what purpose it served, I would be grateful if they would share it with us. A large amount of water gushes from between the rocks at the foot of the monument, and the villagers drink it. The area surrounding the monument is entirely a plateau, and the air was wonderful.

The exact coordinates of the mausoleum are:

38 D 46′ 10” N

36 D 08′ 44” E
