Osman Begi Castle

Little remains of this castle, built on the banks of the Norduz River, east of the Çatak district. Today, only a hut sits on top. Villagers in the area tell stories of how the castle housed the Osman Begi tribe for many years. Built on the edge of a high rock, it commands a commanding position in the region. At its base, only the foundations of a few houses remain, along with a village fountain and a cemetery further up. Nestled among trees and man-height meadows, the castle offers a breathtakingly beautiful sight. It is known in the region by names such as Kasr and Kasrik.
