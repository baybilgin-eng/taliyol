House of the Virgin Mary

Notes from our visit to the House of the Virgin Mary:

After visiting Ephesus, you can easily find it by following the signs.

The house is situated on top of a dominant hill.

This is a house, and according to written accounts, the Virgin Mary is said to have resided here for a period of time.

Taking photos inside the house is forbidden, there is constant security, and a Christian clergyman is also present.

You have to pay to visit, but according to what's written, the municipality collects the money, meaning the foundation that owns the house doesn't charge anything.

Inside the house, there is a fresco on the wall that is said to depict the Virgin Mary smiling.

Nestled amidst greenery, in a quiet, peaceful forest, this is a small structure with stone walls.
