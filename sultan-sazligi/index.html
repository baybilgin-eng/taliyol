Kayseri & Koramaz · 13 June 2014

Sultan Reedbed

While you can find hundreds of pages of information about this reed bed online, I'd also like to share my observations with you.

Sultan Sazlığı, which you can reach via the Yahyalı – İncesu highway (Ova Çiftliği Village), is a veritable paradise for birds.

The water level in the reed bed decreased significantly between 2000 and 2009, but in 2009, water transported from the Zamantı River by tunneling through the mountains revived the reed bed, and the water level is currently at a satisfactory level.

Sultan Sazlığı is a massive national park covering 24,000 hectares (39,000 hectares including the protected area).

A small museum and visitor information center has been built at the entrance to the reed bed. Inside, you can see models of the bird species living in the area, examine a bird's-eye view map of the region, watch a video of the reed bed filmed by a glider, and see birds live with cameras installed in the reed bed. (3 TL per person)

Right across from this museum is a restaurant and guesthouse called Sultan Pansion, and I met the owner, Atalay Aksoy.

Mr. Atalay explained that he has been serving in the same place with the same purpose since 1986, and he described the history and changes of the region.

He is a true bird enthusiast and also provides professional bird guiding services. (0538 202 26 29 – 0535 336 51 88)

In addition to birds, the reedbeds are also home to wildlife such as wild boars, snakes, fish, wolves, jackals, and foxes.

Vehicles are prohibited from entering the reedbeds; there is a special platform accessible on foot, 1.2 km long, which allows you to explore the reeds, observe birds, and reach two small lakes. At the end of the path, there are two observation huts.

Another suggested way to explore the reedbeds is by boat. At Sultan Pasion, local operators offer boat tours with guides, lasting 2-3 hours and costing 120 TL.

Birdwatching and photography is quite a difficult and time-consuming activity requiring great patience. Don't go to the reedbeds expecting to see hundreds of bird species; the birds are very good at hiding, and this area is 24,000 hectares!

If you are very patient and wait quietly for hours without moving, or if you go for a boat ride, you might spot a few beautiful species.

Location: 38°14'39.48″N, 35°11'53.58″E
