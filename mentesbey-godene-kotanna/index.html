Menteşbey – Gödene – Kotanna

Menteş Bey is a modern and large village settlement located in the Akseki district of Antalya.

Its old name was Gödene, and its even older name was Kottana (Kotannâ).

The Luwians, an ancient people, lived in this region for a long time, and the remains of Luwian fortresses on the high hills can still be seen today.

Another important feature of the village is that it was the place that provided the most judges to the Ottoman Empire; almost everyone in the village served as a judge.

The Menteşbeyli judges who served as judges were well-to-do individuals, and therefore employed Arab servants; today, many villagers are married to Arab servants.

The village is situated in a valley opening to the Mediterranean Sea, therefore its climate is wonderful and almost everything grows there. To give a few examples: Olives (the Luwians were involved in olive cultivation here in the past, and there are ancient olive processing facilities in the village), Baghdad (Japanese) dates, jujubes, pomegranates, walnuts, figs, mandarins, oranges,…

The village has carried its past richness into the present, with all the village roads paved with cobblestones.

When going from Seydişehir to Akseki, you turn onto the İbradı road, and after a few kilometers, you turn at the Menteşbey sign.
