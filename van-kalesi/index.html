Van Castle

Everyone knows that Van Castle is an Urartian fortress. After the Urartians, whichever nation ruled Van, they always used this castle. With the Republican era, the castle transformed into a tourist attraction. The area surrounding the castle is full of water sources; even a spring called Hor Hor emerges from within. Due to its caves and the cascading sound of water, Van Castle has been the setting for numerous legendary stories. Evliya Çelebi mentions Van Castle at length in his travelogue. Currently, restoration work is underway, returning the castle to its Ottoman-era state. A restored mosque, open for worship, is located at the summit of the castle. Numerous other mosques are also undergoing restoration at the foot of the castle, and a beautiful old Van house stands at the castle entrance. It's well worth a visit to this castle, whose beauty is beyond description.
