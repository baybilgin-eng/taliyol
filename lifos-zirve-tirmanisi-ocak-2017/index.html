Kayseri & Koramaz · 8 June 2017

Lifos Summit Climb (January 2017)

I've mentioned Lifos Mountain before; located north of Erciyes Mountain and southeast of Hacılar district, it's also called Barut Mountain. With its very steep slope, climbing this mountain is truly enjoyable. I've climbed to its summit many times before, but the climb I'm about to describe was my first time reaching the summit during the winter months.

We parked our car at the Hacılar ski resort, then took the cable car to the upper station. From there, we started walking towards Mount Lifos. Since there was no snow in the city center, we assumed there would only be a few centimeters of snow during the hike, so we had prepared inadequate clothing. Unfortunately, we were greeted by a snow cover reaching up to 2 meters in places, making for an incredibly tiring and difficult climb.

Both our legs were completely buried in the snow; after pulling one out, I would use my knee to lift the other leg out of the snow and then take another step. At one point, another teammate tried to make progress by rolling horizontally, but that was also very tiring. We identified a rock at the foot of Lifos as a resting point and proceeded to that point. At this point, I was let down by my unsuitable clothing for the snow; my trousers were completely wet and even frozen, melting again as they touched my body and taking all the heat from me. My boots were also filled with snow, and my socks were soaking wet. I couldn't continue like this. I took off my trousers and decided to continue the climb in shorts. After taking off my trousers, I had warmed up somewhat; the sun was shining and there was no wind, so I took advantage of this and climbed vertically up to Lifos from the steepest point. We didn't encounter any animals on the way except for one or two foxes.

We rested a bit at the Lifos summit and took some photos. After a few pictures, my phone froze due to the cold, a terrible frost started, so we quickly began our descent. We trudged through the snow, making our way down the steep slope.

By the time we got to the car, I couldn't feel my feet and legs.

I experienced one of the most challenging climbs of my life.

Lessons to be learned:

If you're going mountain climbing in winter, be sure to wear snowproof clothing and waterproof boots.

Wear goggles and thermal underwear.

Bring a thermos of hot drinks with you.

Bring energy-boosting foods like chocolate.

Plan your route beforehand and stick to it.

Bring a spare set of clothes.
