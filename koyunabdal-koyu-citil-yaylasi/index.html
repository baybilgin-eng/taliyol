Kayseri & Koramaz · 8 June 2017

Koyunabdal Village, Çitil Plateau

We actually went to the Koyunabdal village plateau in the Bünyan district of Kayseri by accident. While visiting an abandoned iron mine in Taçın, we spotted houses, but it was such a place that it seemed impossible for people to live there. Overwhelmed by curiosity, we went closer to these houses. As we approached, the scenery became even more interesting, as the houses transformed into a very well-organized village. Yes, during our visit, it was a plateau village where not a single person lived, probably only used during the summer months. I should mention that the transportation is terrible. There are many fountains in the village, and fresh water flows constantly. There is also a cemetery in the village, and some of the tombstones are very old. Interestingly, there is a public toilet and a security booth in the village. All the houses are made of stone and are very old; some are completely ruined, some are in a dilapidated state, and some have locked doors with household items inside. There is no electricity, telephone, or GSM in the village. This abandoned village frankly frightened me. I recommend visiting it for an exciting experience.
