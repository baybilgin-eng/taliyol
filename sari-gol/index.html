Kayseri & Koramaz · 13 June 2014

Yellow Lake

After turning off the road from Hacılar onto the Erciyes Mountain Milk-Freezing Plateau, we continue our journey tirelessly.

The road, winding its way through the highlands, eventually forks; the road to the left, continuing upwards, leads to Süt Donduran Plateau, while the road to the right, continuing without ascending, leads to Sarı Göl (Yellow Lake).

You can reach the lake on foot or by jeep, but the road is quite rough.

The lake's name comes from the reeds that grow in it and spread across the water, with their yellowish hues.

There are wild horses along the shores of the lake; you need patience and luck to see them, as their numbers vary between ten and fifteen, and they only come to the water's edge when they are thirsty.

These horses, whose numbers are dwindling, are among the rare breeds that thrive in their natural habitat.

The lake is not very deep and its surface area is not very large, but the water is very cold.

Approach the water and look carefully inside; you will see dozens of different species of creatures, from crustaceans to crustaceans, dozens of freshwater animals have found life in this lake.

I don't know the names of these species; if anyone knows, I'd appreciate it if they could share them.
