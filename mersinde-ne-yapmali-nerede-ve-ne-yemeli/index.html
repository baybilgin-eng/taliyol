Lake Van Basin · 11 March 2014

What to do in Mersin? Where and what to eat?

Following our trip and short vacation to Mersin, I will share my observations and experiences with you.

First of all, if you intend to visit the historical and touristic areas of Mersin, I recommend staying in the Narlıkuyu region, as it is located right next to many historical sites. There are many hotels in the area; we stayed at the Tolya Hotel, a boutique hotel. The hotel was very clean, but a bit old, but we still liked it. Since it was winter, we stayed for 25 TL per person, including breakfast. We were a little cold because the hotel was heated with air conditioning; the temperature was 14 degrees Celsius at night and 26 degrees Celsius during the day in February. The hotel was located right next to the sea.

Places we visited during our 2-day mini-vacation:

Asthma Cave: We parked in front of a tourist facility and, while looking for the cave, learned that it was accessible via a metal staircase winding through the facility. We paid 3 TL per person and descended meters deep (I think about 80 steps). The cave was full of amazing stalactites, and it was hot and humid; it made us sweat like a sauna.

Heaven-Hell Cave: This area is located very close to the Asthma Cave. A huge sinkhole is divided into two sections, one called Heaven and the other Hell. It requires ample time to explore; it's a very interesting place. In this area, you can take photos with camels, shop, and eat local specialties like tantuni.

Narlıkuyu Mosaic Museum: Located in the center of Narlıkuyu, don't expect to find hundreds of mosaics here; only one floor mosaic is on display. The museum was built on top of the mosaic to avoid moving it, and the mosaic is remarkably well-preserved and features artistic motifs. Note that visiting the museum is free.

Maiden's Castle: Located right next to the Narlıkuyu – Erdemli highway, part of this castle is on the mainland and part is on an island very close to the shore. The beach, with its fine sand and palm trees, is a wonderful place for taking photos. I didn't have time to visit the castle, so I can't write about it in detail, but it's worth seeing.

Elaiussa Ancient City: Located on the Narlıkuyu – Erdemli highway, this ancient city consists of two parts; one part features mosaic-paved floors, while the other contains an ancient theater. With its gardens filled with orange trees, this ancient city is well worth a visit.

Silifke Castle: Located in the center of Silifke district, the castle is built on a dominant, high hill and offers a bird's-eye view of all of Silifke.

Adam Kayalar: All the places I've described so far are located along the main road, but to reach Adam Kayalar you need to turn off the main road and travel 15 km. Furthermore, the entire road isn't paved, and to make matters worse, you have to walk through steep cliffs all the way to the valley floor. The rock carvings visible from the valley floor were quite impressive.

Orange Groves: While in Mersin, you should definitely see the orange groves, especially in the Silifke region.

Akçakıl Bay: After passing Taşucu on the Silifke – Anamur highway, you turn left when you see the Akçakıl sign. This bay, with its calm turquoise blue water paved with large white stones, is breathtaking. I imagine watching the sunrise here would be delightful. Camping and eating fish at the restaurant are also possible here.

What did we eat? What should we eat?

Yaprak Tantuni: They have branches in Erdemli and Mersin, their tantuni was delicious, I recommend it.

Ciğerci Apo: Located in the center of Mersin, near the Forum shopping center, their liver is fantastic. They serve 10 skewers of liver and come with all sorts of side dishes.

Halil the Ice Cream Shop: Located directly opposite Apo the Liver Restaurant, they are famous for their cezerye (a type of Turkish dessert), you can get pistachio or hazelnut cezerye. They also say they make the best kerebiç, a dessert unique to Mersin, so you should try that while you're there. This is the right place for cezerye as a souvenir.

We couldn't go, but I'll go to these places next time.
