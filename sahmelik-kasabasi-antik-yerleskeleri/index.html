Kayseri & Koramaz · 5 June 2014

Ancient Settlements of Şahmelik Town

Located in the Develi district of Kayseri province, the town of Şahmelik lies southeast of Develi on the Develi-Bakırdağı road and is home to a significant number of rock tombs and ancient settlements.

As you pass through the town, you will see a sign guiding you along the main road. Follow this sign, leave the main road, and you will be directed by a second sign; this road will take you directly to the rock tombs and ancient settlements.

Our first stop is the Gölceğiz rock tombs. Around 20 rock tombs of varying sizes are located within these rock walls. I couldn't find any symbols or cultural markers; the tombs are lined up side-by-side, each with its own entrance, chimney, and a raised stone platform inside (presumably where the deceased were placed). I don't know which civilization they belong to, but I've heard that the Hittites and Phrygians lived in the Develi basin, so they could belong to one of these two peoples. Above the rock tombs, I saw carved stone blocks on the ground resembling a water channel.

After visiting the rock tombs of Gölceğiz, the road led us to Kuşluk, where I saw very large birdhouses. I also saw perhaps 30 more rock tombs right next to them.

Our next stop was an ancient settlement whose name I don't know. It was a city carved into a rock block about 200-300 meters long and 20 meters high. It was possible to enter from one side and exit from the other, and inside I saw numerous underground food storage spaces of varying sizes.

The next stop was an ancient settlement called the Staircase Hanging City, accessed by a long iron staircase, which resembled the previous one.

We turned back because we couldn't go any further by car; according to the shepherds, similar settlements continued in the valley further on.

If you're thinking of going, I'm sharing photos and a map below, and I recommend going with a high-clearance vehicle (the road is rough).
