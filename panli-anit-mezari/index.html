Kayseri & Koramaz · 5 June 2015

Persek Rock Settlement and Rock Tombs (Asmalı Dere)

Kapukaya village, formerly known as Persek village, located within the borders of Tomarza district in Kayseri, has been home to ancient civilizations for many years. I set out to trace the remnants of these civilizations, and together with Ali Osman Bey, whom I met at the village coffeehouse, we went to the area that the villagers call Asmalı Dere to examine the rock settlements and rock tombs.

The area known as Asmalı Dere is actually a very narrow and shallow valley, containing numerous man-made rock formations of varying sizes. These structures include sheepfolds, cisterns, dwellings, and rock tombs.

The structures that interested me most, and that bear the traces of ancient civilizations, were the rock tombs. Apparently, human busts and inscriptions once stood on either side of the entrances to the rock tombs. Today, unfortunately, the busts are completely shattered, although some of the inscriptions remain. Inside the rock tombs, there are altars arranged in a U-shape where the deceased and gifts were placed. We also saw two-story, multi-roomed rock structures.

The region possesses a highly valuable cultural heritage, and its preservation and transmission to future generations, at least in its current state, is of paramount importance.
