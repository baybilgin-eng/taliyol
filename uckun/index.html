Rhubarb, also known by various names such as Uçkun, Fışkın, Uçgun, and Işkın, grows in the mountains of Van, Bitlis, and Hakkari, and is called "Rhubarb" in Europe and America. It grows to a height of 10-50 cm among broad green leaves. It is said to be beneficial for diabetes, kidney stones, and stomach ailments. Uçkun grows on the slopes of mountains every year in May-June. It has tufts resembling seeds, and contrary to popular belief, it reproduces from roots, not seeds. The roots of the Uçkun penetrate very deep into the soil, and it is claimed that its growth on slopes prevents erosion. Although attempts have been made to cultivate it in cities by removing its roots, these have been unsuccessful. With its tart taste, Uçkun is popular among the people. Because it is eaten peeled, it is sometimes referred to as "mountain banana," a comparison to a regular banana. It is collected by shepherds and villagers and sold in bundles to the public in the city.
