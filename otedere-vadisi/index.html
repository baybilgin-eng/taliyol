Kayseri & Koramaz · 14 April 2011

Ötedere Valley

Ötedere Valley is located next to Gesi, south of the İldem housing estates, between Belağası (Balagesi) and Kuruköprü. The valley can be reached via the highway connecting Kayseri to Gesi. At the entrance to the valley is a village with only a few houses. Inside the valley lies an underground structure surrounded by thick walls; according to legend, it was also used as a leprosy hospital. For this reason, the valley is also called "Leprosy Valley" by the local people.

A stream flows through the valley. The fact that the stream dries up in mid-summer suggests it is fed by melting snow. The valley is approximately 14 km long and contains numerous ancient settlements.

The most important of these settlements is the "Hasan Efengi Underground City". It is said to have seven levels, and I personally descended to the first three; narrow corridors and small rooms are interconnected. The remains of two bridges, now in ruins, that once connected the two sides of the valley are also located here.

During my visit, I saw that numerous illegal excavations were taking place in the area. I had the opportunity to examine several artifacts unearthed as a result of these excavations, and a marble column capital in particular caught my attention; I believe it may date back to the Roman or Byzantine period.

Like many underground cities in the region, this one is believed to date back to prehistoric times. It is not surprising that ancient civilizations, skilled in stonework, would have carved out such complex underground cities.

According to the villagers, there are areas in the valley where illegal bees produce honey, and every year the villagers harvest lots of delicious honeycombs from there.

There is at least one church in the valley, and in addition, near the directly swallowed waterfall, there is an underground city and a monumental burial site. This burial site contains texts written in the Greek alphabet.

The valley stretches in a north-south direction, and on its western bank lies an area that I believe to be an ancient quarry. This is due to the presence of regularly cut and stacked stones in that area.

The valley contains 3-4 waterfalls of varying sizes. These waterfalls flow particularly strongly during the spring months. It appears that the water flowing through the valley was channeled by ancient settlers through special canals leading into an underground city.

In the autumn months, the valley is full of hawthorn berries, or "yumuşan" as they are known in the east. These red, yellow, and orange berries are fleshy and juicy, leaving a slightly tart taste in the mouth of those who eat them.

A tumulus sits atop the eastern slope of the valley. Its owner and construction date are unknown, but it is known to be a frequent target for treasure hunters.
